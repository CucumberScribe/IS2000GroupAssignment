Our Care Policy

It is our duty to provide the the best quality care that we can. As an animal shelter, it is important to have clear and concise policies on animal care to ensure the safety and well-being of the animals under our wing.

Our general care policy includes:

  1. Vaccinations: All animals in our care must be up-to-date on their vaccinations.
  2. Medical care: We provide necessary medical care to all animals in our care. This includes routine check-ups, spaying/neutering, and any necessary treatments or medications.
  3. Exercise and enrichment: All dogs in our care are given daily exercise and enrichment activities to promote their physical and mental health. Cats and other small animals are provided with appropriate ammenities such toys and direct to keep them stimulated.
  4. Nutrition: We provide high-quality food and water to all animals in our care.
  5. Adoption policies: All potential adopters are thoroughly screened to ensure that they are able to provide a safe and loving home for the animal they wish to adopt. We reserve the right to refuse adoption to anyone who we feel is not a good fit for the animal.
  6. Euthanasia policy: We will only euthanize an animal as a last resort, and only if it is necessary to relieve suffering or if the animal poses a danger to itself or others.
  7. Animal cruelty policy: We have a zero-tolerance policy for animal cruelty and abuse. Any suspected cases will be reported to the appropriate authorities.

As a show of good faith, we receive regular audits and provide statistical data to demonstrate our diligence in maintaining these policies.
